3D assets are a powerful tool for science communication. They make ecological concepts more accessible and engaging, helping share research beyond the scientific community. Thanks to open-source software like Blender and online repositories, producing these visualizations is more achievable than ever.

Visualising Unseen Journeys

Beyond 3D models, data-driven animations can also help bring complex ecologicaldatasets to life, turning numbers and tracks into dynamic stories. By revealing patterns of behaviour, habitat use, and movement, these visualisations make scientific discoveries more tangible, captivating, and easier to understand for researchers, policymakers, and the public alike. Such results can be achieved using platforms like R, in combination with video editing tools, to produce polished, compelling visual content.

Example: reconstructed movement of tagged meagre (Argyrosomus regius) in the Iberian Peninsula.

Image-triggered AR experience

This example shows marker-based AR, where a physical object (like this scientific poster) acts as a trigger. By scanning the poster, a viewer can unlock a hidden digital layer—videos, 3D models, or data visualizations. This approach connects physical outreach materials directly to dynamic digital content, enriching events like scientific conferences or museum exhibits.

Scientific poster presented at the 8th International Biologging Symposium (Tokyo, Japan).

How to try it (Desktop):

  1. Scan the QR Code

    Use your phone's camera to scan this code. This will open the AR experience in your mobile browser.

  2. Point at the Poster

    Once the camera feed opens, point your phone at the poster image on the left side of your screen.

  3. See the Poster Come to Life

    The poster will unlock a hidden digital layer, showing videos and 3D models directly on your screen.

Translating Science Through Digital Media

Each example on this page follows three guiding principles for effective and responsible science communication in ocean research.

Science First

Every visualisation and AR model begins with robust, peer-reviewed data. By grounding the work in rigorous movement ecology research, we ensure that accessibility never comes at the expense of scientific accuracy.

Engagement Through Interactivity

Interactive digital tools can transform how we experience ecological data. Whether through Augmented Reality (AR) or animated data-driven visuals, our goal is to make the underlying science more engaging, interpretable, and memorable for diverse audiences.

Open and Reproducible

All materials are created using open-source tools — from spatial analyses in R to 3D modelling in Blender and browser-based WebXR frameworks. This approach promotes transparency, reproducibility, and broader collaboration across the marine science community.
